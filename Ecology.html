Habitat Restoration, Biodiversity Conservation, and Climate Adaptation

Securing the Future of Life on Earth

The biodiversity crisis is deeply tied to climate change. Protecting and restoring ecosystems strengthens resilience, stores carbon, and safeguards the web of life on which we depend.

Core Strategies

  • Scale up habitat restoration: Restore wetlands, grasslands, forests, rivers, and coasts with native species and soil regeneration.
  • Protect biodiversity hotspots: Expand protected areas and support Indigenous-led conservation.
  • Plan for climate adaptation: Use climate projections, assisted migration, and genetic conservation.
  • Promote connectivity: Build wildlife corridors, crossings, and buffers to reduce fragmentation.
  • Advance regenerative agriculture: Support agroecology and on-farm biodiversity.
  • Halt species loss: Strengthen endangered species protections and fund recovery plans.

Why It Matters

Healthy ecosystems provide carbon storage, water regulation, soil stability, and resilience to climate extremes. Without large, connected, and biodiverse landscapes, both human and nonhuman life are at grave risk.

Integrated Approach

This strategy aligns with land use reform, nature-based climate solutions, agricultural reform, and global goals like protecting 30% of lands and waters by 2030.
