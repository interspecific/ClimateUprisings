Reimagining Society

Degrowth, Humanism, and Environmental Stewardship

To build a just and sustainable future, we must move beyond extractive growth toward an economy centered on well-being and care. This vision redefines abundance and centers stewardship over exploitation, aligning with frameworks like the Doughnut Economy and Planetary Boundaries.

Core Principles

  • Redefine progress: Replace GDP with measures of well-being, equity, and resilience.
  • Prioritize care and time: Shorten work weeks, expand universal basic services, and elevate care work.
  • Democratize wealth and power: Tax wealth, expand public ownership, and support worker cooperatives.
  • Promote sufficiency: Support local food systems, durable goods, zero-waste design, and right-to-repair policies.
  • Embed ecological ethics: Embrace interdependence, regeneration, and reciprocity in all systems.

This visionary jobs program envisions a modern Civilian Climate Corps. A bold initiative that offers meaningful, dignified work while confronting the interlocking crises of climate change, inequality, public health, and ecological collapse.

Core Strategies

  • Establish a Civilian Climate Corps: Employ millions directly in ecological restoration, regenerative agriculture, remediation, green infrastructure, and public transit development.
  • Guarantee fair wages and universal benefits: Living wages, universal basic healthcare, mental health support, paid leave, and retirement—regardless of background or history.
  • Center equity and inclusion: Prioritize hiring in environmental justice communities, fossil fuel-dependent regions, Tribal nations, rural areas, and among formerly incarcerated or underserved individuals.
  • Support holistic wellbeing: Include optional substance use counseling, judgment-free recovery support, therapy, and pathways to stability without stigma or surveillance.
  • Protect from corporate capture: Designed as a public good insulated from private sector exploitation, but open to partnerships with nonprofits, cooperatives, and community-based organizations.
  • Mobilize for sustainability: Focus on decarbonization, walkable cities, ecological clean-up, urban tree planting, public transit, soil regeneration, and climate-resilient infrastructure.
  • Revitalize food systems: Promote local food sovereignty, agroecology, community gardens, and transition away from CAFOs and monoculture toward plant-based, regenerative systems.
  • Funded through climate justice: Tax ultra-wealthy elites and corporate polluters who have profited from planetary degradation—redirecting wealth into healing, care, and green innovation.

Integrated Strategy

This program serves as a foundation for a more just society. It connects climate action with public health, housing, transportation, and food systems. It heals people and planet. It rebuilds trust, belonging, and shared purpose. And it does all of this while preparing the U.S. for global climate leadership and domestic resilience.

Stabilizing the climate and safeguarding life on Earth requires rapidly phasing out fossil fuels and transforming our energy, industrial, and infrastructure systems. This shift must be equitable, science-aligned, and driven by the people—not profit. That means embracing community-owned public power, ensuring a just transition for workers, and embedding social and ecological justice into every phase of decarbonization.

Key Strategies

  • Phase out fossil fuels: End new oil and gas permits, revoke fossil fuel subsidies (which exceed $7 trillion globally per year, according to the IMF), and establish binding emissions caps in line with the IPCC’s call to cut global emissions by 45% by 2030 and reach net-zero by 2050. [IPCC AR6] [IMF]
  • Build a 100% renewable public energy system: Scale wind, solar, geothermal, and long-duration energy storage through public investment and democratic governance. This includes microgrids, municipal utilities, and Indigenous-led energy sovereignty. [NREL]
  • Decarbonize heavy industry: Impose strong emissions standards on steel, cement, aluminum, and chemicals. Invest in industrial electrification, carbon-free fuels, and material circularity. Green hydrogen has major potential—if sourced from renewables. [IEA] [DOE]
  • Electrify buildings and transportation: Replace gas appliances, upgrade building insulation, and roll out electric heat pumps nationwide. Transition public transit, school buses, and delivery fleets to electric—all while ensuring equitable access and clean air for historically overburdened communities. [RMI] [EPA School Bus Program]
  • Support fossil fuel communities: Ensure a just transition by guaranteeing wage replacement, retraining, local economic diversification, and democratic control over reinvestment. Workers and communities must be co-authors of the energy transition. [Brookings] [CJA]

Fossil fuels are responsible for over 75% of global greenhouse gas emissions. They are also at the root of air pollution, cancer clusters, environmental racism, and energy poverty. Building a fossil-free future means cleaner air, lower energy bills, local jobs, and healthier communities. Public energy models—like those in Nebraska, Sacramento, and New York—already show that democratic ownership of energy works.
